POUNDED YAM AND EGUSI SOUP

Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup is a classic Nigerian dish that combines smooth, fluffy pounded yam with a flavorful soup made from melon seeds (egusi), leafy vegetables, and assorted meats or fish.

    INGREDIENTS

  • 1 medium-sized yam, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 cups melon seeds (egusi), ground
  • Assorted meats and fish (beef, goat meat, tripe, stockfish, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, blended
  • 2-3 scotch bonnet peppers, blended (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups spinach or bitter leaf, washed and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 stock cubes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

STEPS

  1. Boil the yam chunks until they are soft. Drain the water and pound the yam until smooth and fluffy. You can use a mortar and pestle or a yam pounder.
  2. In a pot, cook the assorted meats and fish with chopped onions, stock cubes, salt, and pepper until well-cooked and tender.
  3. In a separate pot, heat palm oil. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  4. Stir in the blended tomatoes and scotch bonnet peppers. Cook until the mixture thickens and the oil separates.
  5. Add the ground egusi, ground crayfish, and about a cup of water. Cook until the egusi is well-cooked and the oil begins to float at the top.
  6. Add the cooked assorted meats and fish to the egusi mixture. Stir well.
  7. Pour in more water if needed to achieve your desired soup consistency.
  8. Add chopped spinach or bitter leaf and cook for an additional 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  9. Adjust seasoning if necessary and simmer for a few more minutes.
  10. Serve the pounded yam alongside the egusi soup. Enjoy!
