NIGERIAN EGUSI SOUP

Nigerian Egusi Soup is a rich and hearty soup made with ground melon seeds, leafy vegetables, and various meats. It's a flavorful and nutritious dish enjoyed across Nigeria.

    INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup melon seeds (egusi), ground
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, tripe, etc.), cooked
  • 2 cups spinach or bitter leaf, washed and chopped
  • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, blended
  • 3 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2-3 scotch bonnet peppers, blended (adjust to taste)
  • 2 stock cubes
  • Salt to taste

STEPS

  1. Heat palm oil in a pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add the blended tomatoes and scotch bonnet peppers. Cook until the mixture thickens and the oil separates.
  3. Stir in the ground egusi and continue cooking for about 5-7 minutes, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  4. Add the cooked assorted meats, ground crayfish, stock cubes, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  5. Pour in enough water to achieve your desired soup consistency. Allow it to simmer for about 20-30 minutes.
  6. Add the chopped spinach or bitter leaf and cook for an additional 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Adjust seasoning if necessary and simmer for a few more minutes.
  8. Remove from heat and serve hot with fufu, rice, or your preferred side dish.
