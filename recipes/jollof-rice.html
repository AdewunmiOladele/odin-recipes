NIGERIAN JOLLOF RICE

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a flavorful and popular dish made with rice, tomatoes, and a blend of spices. It's a festive and delicious meal enjoyed at celebrations and family gatherings.

    INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 red bell peppers, blended
  • 4 tomatoes, blended
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: Cooked chicken, beef, or fish for serving

STEPS

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
  3. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the blended red bell peppers and tomatoes. Cook for about 10 minutes until the mixture thickens and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Add bay leaves, thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  6. Stir in the rinsed rice and cook for an additional 5 minutes, ensuring the rice is well coated with the tomato mixture.
  7. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth. Cover the pot and simmer on low heat until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. Optional: Serve with cooked chicken, beef, or fish.
  9. Fluff the rice with a fork, remove bay leaves, and serve hot.
